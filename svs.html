Last Z SVS Guide — State vs State, Capital Clash, and Reward Boxes

PvP Event Guide • Updated February 1, 2026 • 12 min read • Last Z Guides • Checked with server 403 players

Quick Answer

Best Last Z SVS strategy for most players: score through the Sunday-Friday prep phase, help your server become the invader, and aim for all 9 personal boxes before focusing too much on the final Capital Clash result.

  1. ① Biweekly event · 4 servers compete, elimination format
  2. ② Prep phase (Sun-Fri) · earn points for invasion rights
  3. ③ Capital Clash (Sat) · main battle, capture enemy capital
  4. ④ 9 personal boxes · 2.25M points for all rewards
  5. ⑤ Valor Medals · exchange for orange equipment

💡 Key goal: Unlock all 9 personal boxes for maximum Valor Medals, because personal rewards matter even if your server loses the match.

🛡️ Prep phase matters: the higher-scoring server becomes the invader and controls the attack tempo on Saturday.

🏰 Capital Clash: Saturday is the main battle day, but weak prep phase performance can decide the war before it starts.

What Is SVS in Last Z?

Event Overview

If you are searching for what SVS means in Last Z, it is the State vs State event: a biweekly cross-server competition built around prep phase points, invasion rights, and Saturday Capital Clash. The strongest servers treat it as both a PvP war and a long-term reward event.

SVS (State vs State) is one of the biggest competitive events in Last Z. Every two weeks, 4 servers are matched together for cross-state battles. Teamwork matters more than individual strength. For the weekly preparation layer that feeds into this kind of event planning, see the Events Schedule and Alliance Duel Guide.

The event has two main components:

  • State Ruler Prep Phase — Sunday to Friday
  • Capital Clash — Saturday (main battle day)

Matchmaking System

4 servers are grouped for a 2-3 week battle until a champion is crowned:

  • Round 1: Random pairings among four servers
  • Round 2: Winners compete for 1st/2nd place
  • Round 2: Losers compete for 3rd/4th place
  • All servers get two matches regardless of Round 1 result

SVS Prep Phase (Sunday-Friday)

How to Become the Invader

The prep phase decides which server becomes the invader. That matters because invaders control the attack tempo, choose when to enter enemy territory, and usually get the cleaner strategic position before Capital Clash begins.

The server with higher prep phase points becomes the Invader. The lower-scoring server becomes the Defender.

Invader advantage: Freedom to teleport into enemy territory and control attack timing.

On Saturday, the Enemy Buster teleport can also give free server movement to the Alliance Duel opponent, which is useful for Capital positioning when the live event rules allow it.

Defender must: Protect their capital; attacks primarily serve as counter-moves requiring fast coordination.

Capital Clash Strategy (Saturday)

Capital Clash is the main battle day, but most players over-focus on Saturday and under-value the prep phase. In practice, a strong prep phase often matters more because it shapes who attacks first and how much pressure the defending server must absorb.

Battle Phases

  1. Preview Phase — Rules and timing explained
  2. Contest Phase — Main fighting happens here
  3. Final Phase — Rewards distributed based on performance

Victory condition: Reach 100% occupation progress on enemy capital, or have highest progress when event ends.

Map Zones

  • Capital (Center) — Main objective, massive control points
  • Turrets (4x) — Fire rocket attacks when controlled
  • Brown Dirt/Mud — Primary fighting ground for HQ placement
  • Green Level 6 Areas — Buffer zones slowing enemy advances
  • Shield Zones — Safe areas for reinforcements and regrouping

If your main concern is whether you should shield before high-risk windows or stay unshielded with a plan, use the Shield Strategy guide.

Points in Capital Clash

Points are awarded when your troops kill enemies OR when your troops get killed — as long as it happens in the right zones (Capital, Turrets).

  • TVT (Team vs Team) battles — Fastest way to earn points defensively
  • Turret control — Points for kills in turret zones
  • Destroying enemy HQs — ~50k points each for lower-level targets
  • Rally attacks — 100k+ points for elite players

Day-Before Preparation

  • Move HQ to brown dirt (Level 24+ power ideal)
  • Stock heals, teleports, and resources
  • Coordinate with alliance on timing and roles
  • Max troops — train non-stop using speedups from resource platforms

Battle Day Rotation

  1. Event start: Reinforce max allies, begin TvT swarm
  2. Mid-event: Snipe 5-10 low HQs for quick points
  3. End: Capital rallies for burst points

Key insight: Even if your server struggles, consistent activity can still unlock all reward boxes. Focus on personal points.

Rewards

Personal Reward Boxes (9 Total)

Requires 2.25 million total points to unlock all boxes:

  • Boxes 1-3: Resources and small diamonds
  • Boxes 4-6: Orange Skill Books, Badge Alloys
  • Boxes 7-9: ~45,000 Valor Medals total

Why Valor Medals Matter

Valor Medals can be exchanged in the Black Market for orange equipment. This is a major power boost and one of the fastest ways to improve gear without spending.

Valor Medal sources: SVS (main source), Saturday Buster Day during Alliance Duel, and Canyon Clash. The Black Market (Merit Shop) is always available — you just need medals to spend.

See our Gear Guide for equipment upgrade priorities.

Alliance VS Bonus

During the same period, another 9 boxes are available through Alliance VS event. Active participation in both events means 18 total reward boxes — double the Valor Medals.

Server Rewards

Top-placing servers receive:

  • Exclusive titles
  • Speedups
  • Hero shards benefiting all members

Common Mistakes

What to Avoid

  • Skipping prep phase — War can be lost before Saturday if your server doesn't earn enough points
  • Going solo — This is a team event; coordinate with alliance
  • Burning out early — Pace yourself, no need to rush at start
  • Forgetting defense — Even invaders need to protect their capital from counter-attacks
  • Ignoring personal points — Even if server loses, you can still unlock all 9 boxes

Pro Tips

  • Join active alliance — R5+ healers provide significant advantage
  • Use faction counters — Wings of Dawn beats Blood Rose (see PvP Guide)
  • Scout before attacking — Check kill count, not just power
  • Hospital doubles Saturday — Attack on Saturday to minimize permanent troop loss
  • Position HQ in brown dirt — Level 24+ power ideal for defense
  • Focus on personal boxes — 2.25M points unlocks all rewards regardless of server result
  • Sync with Alliance VS — Double your Valor Medals by participating in both events
  • Coordinate timing — Simultaneous attacks overwhelm defenders

FAQ

What is SVS in Last Z?

SVS means State vs State, a biweekly cross-server event where servers compete through a Sunday-Friday prep phase and a Saturday Capital Clash battle.

How does a server become the invader in SVS?

The server with higher prep phase points becomes the invader. Prep points come from activities like Alliance Duel performance, Arena Champion, Furylord damage, and truck raids.

What are Valor Medals used for?

Valor Medals are mainly spent in the Black Market (Merit Shop) for orange equipment and related rewards. SVS is one of the main long-term medal sources.

Can I get rewards if my server loses?

Yes. Personal reward boxes are based on your individual points, not server performance. Focus on earning 2.25M total points to unlock all 9 boxes.

What is the best SVS strategy for F2P players?

For most F2P players, the best SVS strategy is to score steadily during prep phase, avoid unnecessary permanent losses, and prioritize all 9 personal boxes over risky low-value fights.

Is prep phase more important than Capital Clash?

Both matter, but prep phase is often underrated because it decides invader advantage. A strong prep phase can make Saturday much easier for your server.

How often does SVS happen?

Every two weeks. Plan your resources and troop training around the SVS schedule.

What's the difference between invader and defender?

Invaders can teleport into enemy territory and control attack timing. Defenders must protect their capital and coordinate counter-attacks quickly.

Before Fighting In SVS

  • Use this page for planning, then check live state rules, timers, shield coverage, and alliance calls before teleporting or attacking.
  • If matchmaking, scoring windows, or war rules change on your server group, follow the live SVS screen first.
  • Do not risk troops for low-value hits if your personal boxes or alliance calls point somewhere else.
