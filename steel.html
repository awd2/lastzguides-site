Last Z Steel Guide - How to Get Steel for HQ31-35

Economy • Updated February 1, 2026 • 6 min read • Last Z Guides • Checked with server 403 players

Quick Answer

Best way to get steel in Last Z: unlock the HQ31-35 stage, prioritize orange bounties, Steel Mine, steel nodes, Hub Shop, Furylord, and active steel events, then save most steel for HQ31-35 before spreading it into Steel Age research.

  1. ① Unlock after HQ30 · Steel appears with HQ31-35 update
  2. ② Use repeatable sources · bounties, mine, nodes, shops
  3. ③ Collect daily sources · bounties, steel mine, hub shop
  4. ④ Gather steel nodes · world map nodes add steady steel
  5. ⑤ Spend on HQ31-35 · Steel Age research is late-game

ℹ️ Core rule: do not plan HQ31-35 around Tyrant unless your live reward screen shows steel. Build steel from bounties, Steel Mine, nodes, shops, Furylord, and events.

🏢 Unlock point: steel starts mattering once HQ31-35 is active on your server.

⚠️ Common confusion: Steel Factory is a faction building, not a source of steel resource.

What Is Steel in Last Z?

Steel is a late-game resource that becomes one of the main progression bottlenecks after HQ30. If you are searching for how steel works, the key point is simple: it is not part of early progression, but it becomes critical once HQ31-35 and late-game research open up.

Steel is easy to confuse with the Steel Factory faction building. The Steel Factory is a Guard of Order building that boosts hero stats, while steel resource is a material you spend on HQ upgrades and late-game research.

When Steel Unlocks

Players usually search for steel because it seems to appear suddenly. In practice, steel becomes relevant once HQ31-35 is active on your server and your account starts moving past the normal HQ30 economy.

HQ30+ Breakpoint

Steel appears after you reach HQ30 and your server unlocks HQ31-35. That is the point when steel is added to upgrade requirements and reward pools. Use the HQ Upgrade Guide to see where steel costs begin.

Steel Age Research

Steel Age is a late-game research tree that opens once your lab is at higher levels. Most players treat it as a post-HQ30 research focus, after core military and economy research is already in place.

Best Steel Sources in Priority Order

Not all steel sources are equal. Orange bounties and server-stage events can give large bursts, while Steel Mine, steel nodes, Hub Shop, Furylord, and recurring rewards help smooth daily progress.

  1. Orange bounty missions — high steel per bounty in late game.
  2. Steel Mine and steel nodes — steady daily income once unlocked.
  3. Hub Shop and seasonal rewards — useful supporting sources.
  4. Furylord — event source when active.
  5. HQ31-35 launch event — temporary steel boost when available on your server.

Do Not Confuse Tyrant Rewards With Steel

Tyrant is still worth doing, but it should not be treated as your steel plan unless your live reward screen shows steel. Use Tyrant for alliance rewards, damage-based rewards, and Power Core progression instead.

If you can’t join live, enable Engage Offline in the Event Center so your troops can auto-join rallies while you’re away.

  • Tyrant runs on a short schedule window and rewards scale with alliance damage.
  • Alliance leaders (R4/R5) can set the start time for the event.
  • Check the live reward screen before counting any event as a steel source.

Steel Factory vs Steel Resource

This is one of the most common steel-related confusions in Last Z. Steel Factory is a faction building tied to Guard of Order bonuses, while steel resource is a late-game material used for progression costs.

The Steel Factory is a Guard of Order faction building unlocked at HQ14. It increases Guard of Order hero attack, defense, and troop capacity via its upgrades. It does not produce steel resource.

If you are upgrading faction buildings for Hall of Honor bonuses, Steel Factory level 20 unlocks the Guard of Order Hall of Honor buffs.

Common Steel Mistakes

  • Counting Tyrant as guaranteed steel — check the live reward screen before planning HQ31-35 steel around it.
  • Ignoring orange bounties — they scale better than lower-tier bounties.
  • Not gathering steel nodes — a free daily source that adds up.
  • Confusing Steel Factory with steel resource — the building is a faction buff, not a producer.

FAQ

When does steel unlock in Last Z?

Steel appears after HQ30 once your server activates the HQ31-35 update. Before that, steel does not show up in costs or rewards.

What is the best source of Steel?

For most players, practical steel sources are orange bounties, Steel Mine or steel nodes, Hub Shop, Furylord, seasonal rewards, and any active HQ31-35 launch event. Do not count Tyrant as a steel source unless your live reward screen shows steel.

Is Steel Factory the same as Steel resource?

No. Steel Factory is a Guard of Order faction building that boosts hero stats. Steel resource is a late-game material used for HQ upgrades and Steel Age research.

Do I need Steel for HQ30?

No. Steel is required for HQ31-35. HQ1-30 use standard resources.

What is Steel Age research?

Steel Age is a late-game research tree that unlocks around the HQ30+ stage once higher lab levels are available.

What should I spend steel on first in Last Z?

For most players, steel should go to HQ31-35 progression first. Steel Age research usually becomes the follow-up priority after your main HQ path is under control.

Before Chasing Steel

  • Open the live HQ31-35, bounty, shop, or event screen before spending diamonds or saved items for steel.
  • Do not confuse Steel Factory with the actual steel resource used for HQ31-35 progress.
  • If your server has not unlocked HQ31-35 yet, prepare speedups and resources instead of forcing steel value early.
