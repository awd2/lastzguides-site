Last Z Alliance Shop Guide — Shields, Teleports, and Speedups

Economy Guide • Updated May 2026 • Alliance credits and shield value • Last Z Guides • Checked with server 403 players

Quick Answer

Best Last Z Alliance Shop priority: use alliance credits for items that prevent expensive mistakes first. Shields and teleports protect progress when timing is bad; speedups are useful only when they support the right upgrade or event day.

  1. ①First·buy 8-hour or 24-hour shields when you need offline protection and the shop has stock
  2. ②Second·keep teleports for war, alliance relocation, rallies, or escaping a bad map position
  3. ③Third·buy speedups only when they match your active bottleneck or Alliance Duel timing
  4. ④Skip·random resource buys unless they solve an immediate upgrade without draining protection credits

What to Buy First

The Alliance Shop is most valuable when it covers urgent items without spending diamonds. Do not treat it as a place to empty credits every time the shop refreshes.

1. Shields When Real Risk Is Coming

8-hour and 24-hour shields are the cleanest first priority when you will be offline during Enemy Buster, SVS pressure, weekend raids, or any period where you are holding exposed resources.

  • Buy when: you need protection soon and the shop has shield stock.
  • Save diamonds: use alliance credits first when shields are available there.
  • Check stock: if shields are missing, ask an R4 or R5 whether restock is possible.

2. Teleports for Movement Problems

Teleports are worth keeping when position matters: joining alliance territory, moving for war, escaping repeated attacks, or getting into the right rally and event position.

  • Buy when: you have a clear movement need.
  • Skip when: you are already positioned safely and are short on credits.

3. Speedups Only With a Plan

Speedups can be good, but only when they convert into real progress at the right time. Buying speedups and spending them immediately on the wrong day is usually worse than waiting.

  • Good use: construction, research, or training bottlenecks you already planned.
  • Best timing: use them on the matching Alliance Duel day when possible.
  • Bad use: buying speedups just because credits are available.

Common Alliance Shop Mistakes

  • Buying small resource packs while exposed: protection and movement usually matter more than a small refill.
  • Spending all credits before raid windows: then you have no clean fallback when shields or teleports are needed.
  • Assuming stock is permanent: shield availability can depend on shop stock and alliance restock behavior.
  • Buying speedups off-plan: speedups are strongest when saved for the correct event day or a known bottleneck.
  • Ignoring your diamond reserve: Alliance Shop items should support the reserve-first diamond plan, not replace planning.

Weekly Buying Checklist

  1. Check current risk: are Enemy Buster, SVS, alliance war, or weekend raids coming?
  2. Check exposed resources: if you are holding a raidable stash, protection beats convenience buys.
  3. Check shop stock: look for 8-hour shields, 24-hour shields, useful teleports, and relevant speedups.
  4. Protect first: buy the shield or teleport before optional items if you cannot stay online.
  5. Plan speedups: buy only if you know where they will be used and when they score.
  6. Leave a buffer: keep enough credits for the next protection or movement problem.

  • Open the Alliance Shop in game before buying; shield stock and restocks can vary by alliance.
  • If 8-hour or 24-hour shields are available, they usually save more than spending diamonds later in a panic.
  • Ask R4 or R5 about restock before using credits on filler items you do not need right now.
