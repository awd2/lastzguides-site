Why HQ Level Matters: Hero Level Cap and Progression

The Formula

If you are asking why HQ matters so much in Last Z, the short answer is that HQ level controls your hero level cap. That makes HQ progression one of the most important long-term power systems in the entire account.

Every HQ level adds +5 to your hero level cap:

  • HQ 1 → Hero cap 5
  • HQ 20 → Hero cap 100
  • HQ 30 → Hero cap 150
  • HQ 35 → Hero cap 175

Why it matters: Higher hero levels = massive power boost. Your arena performance, overall power, and combat strength all increase significantly. See our Hero Tier List for which heroes to prioritize, the Arena Guide for daily rank value, and the Power Guide for the bigger account-growth picture.

Future-Proofing Note

HQ35 is useful future-proofing on older servers because it raises hero cap to 175 and leaves your account better positioned for later server unlocks. Do not rush HQ35 only because of unconfirmed unit timing.

Sophia — The Queen of Construction

Why Sophia is Essential

Sophia's 3rd skill gives you:

  • Extra free construction duration
  • Faster construction speed
  • At 4 stars: Resource cost -5%
  • At 5 stars: Resource cost -10%

Quote: "In a game where upgrading costs hundreds of millions and eventually billions, these discounts save you a mountain of resources. It's like having a personal accountant who actually knows what they're doing."

The Rule

Try to have Sophia at 5 stars before the expensive HQ30+ push.

Her bonus applies to every building. Since the HQ path forces many prerequisite upgrades, Sophia saves resources at every step. Build her early if you are committed to long-term HQ progression.

Fastest HQ Upgrade Path: Levels 1-35

The fastest HQ path is not upgrading everything evenly. The efficient route is to rush the early levels, only build what the next HQ checkpoint requires, and save bigger resources and speed-ups for the levels where timers and costs start to spike.

Phase 1: HQ 1-18 (Fast & Easy)

Upgrading is smooth, fast, and cheap. You feel like a building genius.

  • Can be done in a few days
  • With bonuses: less than 2 days possible
  • Costs stay low compared with later HQ checkpoints

Tip: Don't slow down here. Rush through these levels.

Phase 2: HQ 18-24 (Reality Check)

Upgrade times get longer. Some take a week or more. Resource costs "punch you in the face."

Strategy: Take advantage of early game chaos. Loot weaker players while you can. Don't tie yourself into too many peace treaties early.

Quote: "The game before Season 1 is all about alliance domination. Pick the right team and start climbing."

Phase 3: HQ 24-27 (Smart Play Required)

Things slow down significantly. You'll feel it.

  • Saving speedups becomes crucial
  • Only upgrade buildings required for next HQ
  • Don't waste resources on random buildings just because they glow with a blue arrow

Priority: HQ first, then only required support buildings.

Phase 4: HQ30 (Major Milestone)

HQ30 is the biggest practical milestone for most players. It unlocks major late-game progression, raises your hero cap further, and marks the point where your account starts preparing for steel-based HQ31-35 upgrades.

  • Required for T10 troops — see Research Priority for tech requirements
  • If HQ31-35 isn't unlocked yet, use this time to prepare
  • Stack resources, speedups, construction bonuses
  • Upgrade Hall of Honor and formation buildings for power boosts

Phase 5: HQ31-35 (Steel Phase)

HQ31-35 is effectively a second progression chapter. Once steel enters the picture, the upgrade path becomes much slower, much more expensive, and far less forgiving if you waste speed-ups or construction bonuses.

These levels are brutally expensive and require a new resource: Steel.

Reality check: the longest unbuffed HQ31-35 timers can run for several months. Use every construction bonus you can before committing to these levels.

Steel — The New Resource

When Steel Appears

Steel only exists after reaching HQ30. Before that, the game acts like steel doesn't exist. Once you hit 30 and unlock the HQ31-35 update, steel suddenly appears everywhere.

For a full breakdown of sources, see the Steel Guide.

How to Get Steel

  • Orange Bounties — high steel per bounty in late game
  • Steel Mine and steel nodes — steady daily income once unlocked
  • Hub Shop and seasonal rewards — useful supporting sources
  • Furylord — event source when active
  • HQ31-35 launch event — temporary steel boost when available on your server

The Special Event

Around Season 3, right after the HQ31-35 update, there's a special event to help players climb from 30 to 31. You earn lots of steel just for daily tasks.

Important: Make absolutely sure you take advantage of that event. The steel makes HQ31 much easier.

Steel Is Only One Bottleneck

Steel adds pressure after HQ30, but it is not the only HQ31-35 blocker. Orange bounties, the Steel Mine, steel nodes, the Hub Shop, Furylord, and server events build your steel supply over time.

The other bottleneck: speedups. Long construction timers can stop progress even when your steel plan is on track.

Alliance Duel Day 2 Strategy

The Pro Tip

Keep your HQ upgrade ready, but do NOT finish it until Alliance Duel Day 2.

  • Start the upgrade anytime
  • When it's ready, you'll see a green checkmark
  • That is the most dangerous button in the game
  • One accidental tap = points gone forever
  • Hold until Day 2, then complete for massive points

Double Bonus

Try to align HQ completion with Full Preparedness on the same day. You get bonus points from both sides. Learn more in our Alliance Duel Guide.

Quote: "Treat that button like a self-destruct switch. Look at it, but do not touch it."

Construction Speed Setup

Stack Every Bonus

For HQ31-35, prepare your full construction speed setup:

  • Villa — gives construction bonuses
  • Refugees for Villa — boost construction speed a lot
  • Sophia 5 stars — reduces costs and speeds up construction
  • Lab buffs — check for construction bonuses
  • Alliance City 4 — make sure your alliance holds it
  • Alliance Lab — prioritize construction research
  • All speedups — collect every one you can get

HQ Upgrade Requirements: What Buildings Actually Matter

Consistent Pattern

From HQ8 to HQ30, Laboratory is mandatory for every upgrade.

Key buildings that must always be kept up:

  • City Walls
  • Alliance Center
  • Assaulter Camp
  • Shooter Camp
  • Rider Camp

From HQ8 to HQ30, this Lab + rotating prerequisite pattern is the core rule. For HQ31-35, check the HQ Construction Cost Table because later requirements and steel costs should be planned level by level.

What NOT to Upgrade Early

Avoid upgrading unless directly required for next HQ:

  • Resource production buildings (tempting but slow you down)
  • Hall of Honor buildings (bonuses too small early, save for later)
  • Random buildings with blue arrows

Optional Early Upgrades (Worth It)

  • Formation buildings
  • Military Center
  • Bookstore
  • Restaurant
  • Production Center
  • Rally buildings
  • Radar

HQ Skins

Types of Skins

  • Temporary — disappear after 1 day
  • Permanent — win from events
  • Permanent with stats — the real luxury

Battle Fortress — Easiest Skin

First skin most players get. Just reach 8 million power, complete a few tasks, and it's yours permanently. If you missed the event, you can buy it later in the shop with tickets.

Other Skins

  • Legendary: DJ Cat, Nightmare (Halloween)
  • Best overall: Dragon — "That thing looks like your HQ is about to take off and start breathing fire on the map"

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the max headquarters level in Last Z?

Level 35 on older servers. Newer states stay at 30 until Season 3 update. Each HQ level adds +5 to hero level cap (HQ35 = hero cap 175).

What is the fastest way to upgrade HQ in Last Z?

Rush the easy early levels, upgrade only the buildings required for the next HQ checkpoint, push efficiently to HQ30, and save major construction bonuses for the steel-heavy HQ31-35 phase.

How do I get steel in Last Z?

Steel only appears after HQ30 once HQ31-35 is active on your server. Best sources are orange bounties, Steel Mine or steel nodes, Hub Shop, Furylord, seasonal rewards, and any active HQ31-35 launch event. Do not count Tyrant as a steel source unless your live reward screen shows steel.

Why is Sophia important for HQ upgrades?

Her 3rd skill reduces ALL building costs by 10% at 5 stars and speeds up construction. In a game with billion-cost upgrades, this saves massive resources on every building.

How long does it take to reach HQ35?

Without bonuses, the longest HQ31-35 timers can run for several months. With a full construction speed setup such as villa, refugees, Sophia, and alliance buffs, they become much faster. Speedups remain the main bottleneck.

Is it worth upgrading past HQ30?

For long-term accounts, usually yes: each HQ level raises hero level cap by +5, and HQ35 reaches hero cap 175 on older servers. Do not rush past HQ30 only because of unconfirmed unit timing; make the push when your resources, steel, and speedups can support it.

Last Z HQ upgrade requirements (including HQ31-35)?

From HQ8 to HQ30, Laboratory is mandatory and the second prerequisite rotates through buildings like City Walls, Alliance Center, Assaulter Camp, Shooter Camp, and Rider Camp. For HQ31-35, use the HQ Construction Cost Table because steel and later prerequisites should be planned level by level.

Why should I stop random building upgrades when pushing HQ?

Because random upgrades slow HQ progression and waste resources on buildings that do not unlock the next checkpoint. The fastest HQ path is almost always requirement-driven, not balanced-building progression.

When do HQ levels 31-35 unlock?

Season 3. The update adds steel as a new resource and a special event helps players climb from 30 to 31 with bonus steel rewards.
